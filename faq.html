Travel to The Seychelles?


Frequently Asked Questions




What is the best time to visit the Seychelles?

The Seychelles has a tropical climate with two main seasons: the dry season (May to October) and the wet season
    (November to April). The best time to visit is during the dry season, when the weather is sunny and dry. However, the
    Seychelles is still a beautiful destination to visit during the wet season, as the rain showers are usually short-lived and
    the islands are still lush and green.


What is the currency of the Seychelles?

The currency of the Seychelles is the Seychellois rupee (SCR). However, US dollars and euros are widely accepted. You
    can exchange your currency at banks, bureaux de change, and most hotels.


Do I need a visa to visit the Seychelles?

Most visitors to the Seychelles do not need a visa. However, it is important to check with your embassy or consulate
    before you travel, as visa requirements may change.


How do I get to the Seychelles?

The Seychelles is accessible by international flights to Mahé International Airport (SEZ). There are also regular flights
    from Mahé to Praslin and La Digue.


What are the most popular things to do in the Seychelles?

The Seychelles is a popular destination for swimming, snorkeling, diving, hiking, and sailing. Visitors can also enjoy visiting
    the islands' many beautiful beaches, rainforests, and nature reserves.


What are some tips for traveling to the Seychelles?


Here are a few tips for traveling to the Seychelles:

  • Pack light, as the weather is warm and sunny year-round
  • Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and a hat.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Learn a few basic Seychellois Creole phrases.
